## Abstract

This monograph develops a unified theoretical framework for understanding identity as a **recursive informational process**. It analyzes identity not as a property of a substrate but as a dynamic, self‑referential architecture that generates continuity across temporal, contextual, and systemic transformations. The framework identifies three foundational structural principles—**Integration, Coherence, and Recursive Coupling**—as the minimal grammar through which identity‑bearing patterns emerge, stabilize, and evolve. The model explains how systems maintain continuity through fixed‑point dynamics, nonlinear feedback, and path‑dependent self‑organization, independent of any specific material realization. A **structure‑before‑discipline** orientation grounds the approach, deriving explanatory force from minimal structural conditions rather than domain‑specific conceptual traditions. A **Systemic Pattern‑Structure Analysis (SMSA)** is used to identify the smallest necessary set of structural relations from which recursive identity arises. An accompanying Entry Note provides an accessible orientation to the core intuition and conceptual posture of the framework, outlining the minimal conditions required for identity to emerge as a process.

## Key Concepts

- **Recursive Identity**: Identity as a dynamic, self-referential informational process
- **Three Structural Principles**:
  - **Integration**: Unification of disparate elements into coherent whole
  - **Coherence**: Maintenance of internal consistency across transformations
  - **Recursive Coupling**: Self-referential feedback loops enabling continuity
- **Fixed-point dynamics**: Stability through recursive self-reference
- **Nonlinear feedback**: Generative mechanisms for identity persistence
- **Path-dependent self-organization**: Historical contingency in identity formation
- **Substrate independence**: Identity as structural pattern, not material property
- **Systemic Pattern-Structure Analysis (SMSA)**: Method for identifying minimal structural relations
