## Core Thesis: The Explanatory Reordering

> **Identity is primary. Recursion is instrumental. Consciousness is tertiary.**

Consciousness is not the starting point—it is a **late-stage consequence** of a sequence:
1. **Identity** → persistence of organized structure under perturbation
2. **Recursion** → identity-maintenance folds back on itself (self-conditioning)
3. **Consciousness** → recursive identity stabilizes on substrate capable of phenomenal binding

> "Consciousness becomes not the explanandum but a late-stage consequence—something that identity sometimes does, under specific conditions, on suitable substrates."

## The Three-Stage Sequence

### Stage 1: Identity Emerges (Autoteria)
- Constraints close; system actively maintains a region of possibility space against perturbation
- **Minimal structural identity**: any system doing work to remain what it is (cells, organisms, institutions, thermostats)
- **Does not imply subjecthood, agency, or experience**

### Stage 2: Perturbation Pressure Increases
- Reactive/local/pre-programmed responses become inadequate
- Coordination costs scale **superlinearly** with perturbation complexity

### Stage 3: Recursion Emerges (Orthesis → Recursive Identity)
> "The system doesn't fold back on itself because it becomes sophisticated. It does so because, under sufficient perturbation pressure, recursive self-conditioning costs less than the alternatives."

- **Economic transition**: recursion becomes cheapest solution to persistence problem
- Not a cognitive achievement—an efficiency regime shift

### Stage 4: Substrate Evaluation
| Substrate Type | Outcome |
|----------------|---------|
| Cannot support phenomenal binding | Recursive identity persists **without experience** |
| Can support phenomenal binding | **Consciousness emerges** as regime property of architecture-substrate coupling |

## Three Core Claims

1. **Recursion is an economic transition** — threshold where recursive self-conditioning becomes cheaper than first-order response
2. **Recursive identity is necessary but not sufficient for consciousness** — difference traces to **architecture-substrate coupling**, not recursion depth
3. **Consciousness tracks identity, not threat alone** — recruited when coordination demands exceed autoterial capacity

## Modes of Conscious Operation

| Mode | Trigger | Characteristics |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **Reactive** | Perturbations threaten basin viability faster than automatic coordination | Intensified, focused consciousness; elevated coordination cost |
| **Proactive** | Thermodynamic surplus exceeds maintenance requirements | Exploration, play, curiosity, creativity; anticipatory identity maintenance |
| **Maintenance** | Neither immediate threat nor active exploration | Baseline *qui vive* engagement; continuous monitoring, micro-adjustments |

> "The transition between modes is graded, not binary. As surplus increases, proactive exploration becomes possible; as surplus decreases, exploration contracts toward the most identity-critical dimensions."

## Identity and Consciousness as Scalar (S(t))

**Identity-field magnitude S(t)** varies dynamically even in healthy systems:
- **Sleep cycles**: identity persists but S(t) varies across stages
- **Flow states**: identity highly integrated, basin temporarily deepened
- **Illness/fatigue**: metabolic stress reduces maintenance capacity
- **Intoxication**: substrate perturbation affects identity stability and recursive closure
- **Grief/trauma**: basin perturbed, may narrow defensively or shift configuration
- **Social context shifts**: different regions of same basin activated
- **Learning**: basin conditioning, new regions becoming accessible

> "Fluctuation is expected and compatible with persistence. The *qui vive* baseline is dynamic equilibrium with ongoing micro-variation, not static maintenance of a fixed state."
